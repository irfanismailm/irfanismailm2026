01 — Essence & voice

Clarity, with craft.

I help people make sense of tech, work, and doing good — and make it look effortless. One curious generalist, four lanes, one consistent voice. Everything in this book protects that: it should read clearly and look considered, every time.

Clear

Plain words, no gatekeeping. If a nine-year-old couldn't follow it, simplify.

Warm

A helpful friend, not a lecturer. Encourage; never talk down.

Generous

Value first, always. Give the useful thing away before any ask.

Crafted

It should look as good as it reads. Details are the brand.

Sounds like
  • Here's the one thing that actually matters.
  • Steal this, it saved me hours.
  • You don't need expensive gear — you need this.
Not like
  • Unlock the ultimate secret nobody tells you!!!
  • As a thought leader in the space…
  • Obviously everyone already knows that…

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space around the signature equal to the height of the capital I. Don't crowd it with other type or graphics.
Minimum size: the signature stays legible down to ~28 px tall on screen. Below that, use the avatar — or, under ~40 px, the I mark.

Titles & kinetic text

Bricolage bold, sentence case, short lines (≤5 words). Emphasise one word with a coral highlight or the pen stroke. Animate with a quick rise/fade (~0.3s) and ease-out — purposeful, never bouncy.

Motion. Quick, purposeful cuts with ease in/out. One signature transition (a clean cut or a soft pen-swipe). Let content lead.
07 — Captions

Write like you talk — usefully.

Every caption earns attention in the first line and pays it back with something useful. Hook, value, one ask.

01

Hook

First line stops the scroll. ≤8 words, a promise or a tension. No "hey guys".

02

Value

Deliver the goods — the tip, story, or steps. Short lines, whitespace, one idea at a time.

03

Ask

One CTA: save it, follow, comment, or tap the link. Never five asks at once.

Why it obeys the rules

71% Paper
20% Ink
9% Coral
  • Every font appears exactly once, in its assigned job. Bricolage speaks, Hanken explains, Space Mono labels, Kaushan signs.
  • Coral turns up three times — highlight, stroke, CTA — but it reads as one accent moment: a single colour drawing a single line from the idea to the thing you should do next.
  • Soft grey and dim carry the supporting text so ink stays reserved for what matters. Hairlines do the dividing; boxes and shadows never appear.
  • Crop marks in ink, the pen stroke in coral, one topic tab. Three graphic elements, no more — the kit is a seasoning, not the meal.

Same poster, four lanes

Nothing moves; only the accent swaps. Coral is the default when a post belongs to no single lane. Change the accent, change the subject — and never mix two in one layout.

Wrong
Six ways this breaks the brand
  • Two accents. An indigo tab and an amber button in one layout. Pick a lane.
  • Kaushan as text. Headline and body set in the signature face. It signs; it never speaks.
  • The signature abused. Stretched, rotated, and given a drop shadow. Scale it proportionally, keep it level, leave it flat.
  • Dark and heavy. The brand is warm paper with soft ink. Contrast comes from restraint, not from a black hole.
  • Body copy in coral. The accent points at one thing. Long text in coral points at nothing.
  • A hashtag wall. Fourteen tags doing the job the design should have done. Three to six, curated, in the first comment.
09 — Quick rules

Do & don't.

Do

  • Lead with value; give before you ask.
  • Keep the signature ink, the stroke coral.
  • Use one accent moment per layout.
  • Make subtitles big, bold, and mobile-first.
  • Tag each post's lane with a topic colour.
  • Stay consistent — same watermark spot, same fonts.
  • Ship one favicon: the Kaushan I with its coral stroke.

Don't

  • Clickbait or over-promise.
  • Recolour or add effects to the signature.
  • Mix two accent colours in one graphic.
  • Wall posts with 30 hashtags.
  • Set body text or paragraphs in Kaushan.
  • Go dark and heavy — the brand stays light.
  • Rebuild the small mark as initials or a monogram.
